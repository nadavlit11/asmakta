# Working Hours and Weekly Rest

## The standard workweek

The corpus uses a standard full-time workweek of 42 hours. Work beyond the daily
or weekly norm is overtime and is paid at premium rates. The workday has a maximum
length, beyond which additional hours are overtime.

## Overtime pay

Overtime is paid at a higher rate than regular hours. In the illustrative rule
used here, the first two overtime hours in a day are paid at 125% of the regular
hourly wage, and each additional overtime hour is paid at 150%. Overtime must be
compensated in pay (or, where permitted and agreed, partly in time off) and cannot
simply be ignored.

## Weekly rest

Every employee is entitled to a weekly rest of at least 36 consecutive hours. For
Jewish employees the weekly rest includes Shabbat; for employees of other faiths
it may fall on Friday, Saturday, or Sunday according to their religion. Work during
the weekly rest is permitted only under a permit and is paid at a premium.

## Breaks during the workday

An employee working a long day is entitled to a break for rest and meals. The break
is generally not counted as working time unless the employee is required to remain
at the employer's disposal during it.

## Night work

Night work is subject to special rules, and the length of a shift that includes
night hours may be shorter than a regular day shift.
