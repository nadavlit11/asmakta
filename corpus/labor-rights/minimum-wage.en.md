# Minimum Wage

## The right to a minimum wage

Israeli law guarantees every employee a minimum wage. An employer may not pay
less than the minimum wage set by law, and any agreement to pay below it is not
valid. The minimum wage is defined both as a monthly amount for full-time
employees and as an hourly rate for hourly workers.

## Monthly and hourly minimum

For a full-time employee, the minimum is expressed as a monthly figure based on
the standard full-time month. For employees paid by the hour, the minimum hourly
wage applies to each hour worked. Part-time employees are entitled to the minimum
hourly wage for the hours they actually work.

## Updates

The minimum wage is updated periodically. When the statutory minimum rises, the
employer must apply the new rate from the effective date. Employees whose pay is
below the updated minimum must have their wages raised accordingly.

## Youth and trainees

Young workers (minors) and certain trainees may be subject to a reduced minimum
wage that is a percentage of the adult minimum, according to their age bracket.
This corpus treats the adult minimum wage as the default unless a question
specifies a minor.

## Enforcement

An employee paid below the minimum wage may claim the difference. The minimum
wage is a protective right and is enforced by law.
