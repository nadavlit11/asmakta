# Annual Leave

## Entitlement

Every employee in Israel is entitled to paid annual leave. The amount of leave
depends on the employee's seniority with the employer. In the illustrative
schedule used by this corpus, an employee is entitled to 12 paid vacation days
per year during the first several years of employment, and the entitlement rises
gradually with seniority to a higher number of days for long-tenured employees.

Annual leave is a mandatory right and cannot be waived in exchange for pay while
the employment relationship continues. Leave is intended to allow rest, and the
employer and employee coordinate its timing.

## Payment during leave

An employee on annual leave receives their regular wage for the leave period —
"holiday pay" (dmei chufsha). Leave days are paid as if the employee had worked
those days.

## Accrual and carryover

Unused leave may generally be accrued and carried over for a limited period,
subject to the rules that apply to the workplace. On termination of employment,
the employer pays the employee redemption (pidyon) for accrued leave days that
were not taken.

## Part-time and hourly employees

Part-time and hourly employees accrue annual leave in proportion to the scope of
their employment. The entitlement is pro-rated relative to a full-time position.
